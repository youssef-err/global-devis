# What Is VAT in Morocco?

If you invoice clients in Morocco, you need to understand VAT (TVA) presentation. Even when your accounting is correct, unclear tax formatting can create confusion for clients and delay payments.

This guide explains VAT basics and invoice best practices in a practical way.

## VAT basics for invoice readability

For business invoices, clients expect to see:

- **Subtotal (HT)**: amount before tax
- **VAT rate (TVA %)**: applicable percentage
- **VAT amount**: calculated tax value
- **Total (TTC)**: final payable amount

This structure should be visible in one compact totals section.

## When VAT is applied

VAT application depends on your legal/tax status and service type. Rules vary by business regime and sector. The key for invoices is:

1. Be explicit about whether VAT applies  
2. Use the right rate  
3. Keep calculations consistent

If VAT does not apply, avoid ambiguous totals. Show clear wording in notes or terms.

## Formula you should always verify

Use this simple logic:

- Subtotal HT = sum of line amounts  
- VAT amount = Subtotal × VAT rate  
- Total TTC = Subtotal + VAT amount (+ fees − discounts)

A clean calculator or invoice tool helps reduce manual errors.

## Invoice presentation tips (Morocco context)

### Use standard labels clients recognize
- Sous-total HT
- TVA (%)
- Montant TVA
- Total TTC

### Keep numbers right-aligned
This matters more than most freelancers think. Finance teams scan columns quickly.

### Keep currency consistent
If you invoice in MAD, keep all lines in MAD and avoid mixed symbols.

### Include tax identifiers when required
If your legal setup requires identifiers (like ICE), place them clearly in sender/client sections.

## Common VAT mistakes on invoices

### Tax hidden in line prices
Clients prefer seeing tax as a separate line for control.

### No VAT percentage shown
Without the rate, clients cannot validate calculations.

### Inconsistent decimal rounding
Even small rounding mismatches cause payment delays in strict finance workflows.

### Mixing HT and TTC labels incorrectly
Be explicit in labels to avoid disputes.

Simple, clear, and audit-friendly.
